Welcome New Runners!

Them Trails equipment and safety guidelines

Whether you're new to trail running or just new to our community, we're thrilled to have you here! Trail running is a fantastic way to connect with nature, challenge yourself, and meet like-minded LGBTQIA2S individuals and allies. As a member of Them Trails, you'll find a supportive and inclusive environment where everyone is welcome.

To get started, fill out the join form on the home page. If you have problems with the form or have questions, join one of our group runs or events listed on our Events page or the monthly calendar, and we can help you. Don't forget to check out our safety tips and the Phoenix Parks Hike Safety Tips link below to ensure you have a safe and enjoyable experience on the trails. If you have any questions or need assistance, feel free to reach out to us via email. Happy trails!

For more safety tips, please check out Phoenix Parks Hike Safety Tips.

Ten safety tips for the trails


  • Bring water. Bring up to a gallon for the run, and don't forget electrolytes.

  • Trail shoes. You need well-fitting trail shoes. Regular running shoes will not be suitable for some of the trails because the rocks are too slick.

  • Bring an ice pack. An ice pack on your pressure point can help cool you fast if needed.

  • Banda or cooling rag. Bring a cooling rag or bandana to help cool you down during the run.

  • Sunscreen. Always use sunscreen, even when it is cloudy, because you can still burn.

  • Bring a snack. Be sure to bring a snack that won't spoil in the heat, like a Clif Bar or fruit.

  • Check the weather. Check the weather so you know how to dress for your run.

  • Lighting. Bring a headlamp for night running, and/or a safety light for visibility.

  • Keep to the trails and be alert. Going off-trail is dangerous, and you can easily get lost when running.

  • Never go alone. The whole point of the theme trails is to connect with people who love to trail run. Splitting off from the group and running alone is dangerous.
